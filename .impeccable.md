## Design Context

### Users

The primary audience is restaurant owners and general prospective Atrium clients evaluating the agency's services. They need to understand what each service does, why it matters commercially, and what the work looks like in practice without needing marketing expertise.

### Brand Personality

Warm, candid, and commercially sharp. The brand should feel close to real independent US hospitality rather than polished stock photography or generic agency SaaS. It should communicate creative confidence without becoming precious or opaque.

### Aesthetic Direction

Combine documentary hospitality photography with purposeful animated graphics, interface moments, data visualizations, and restrained motion. Photography follows the established disposable-camera language: direct flash, visible grain, warm amber highlights, deep teal shadows, candid framing, and real restaurant environments. Graphic and motion elements should clarify service mechanics and outcomes while preserving that tactile, human character.

### Design Principles

- Show the work in context: every visual should demonstrate a service, workflow, or outcome rather than decorate a card.
- Mix media with intent: photography establishes emotion; diagrams, UI, and motion explain the system behind it.
- Reserve movement for cause-and-effect, transformation, or sequence; avoid constant ambient animation.
- Keep concepts legible to non-marketers through recognizable restaurant scenarios and plain visual metaphors.
- Vary bento compositions across the page so the section feels editorial rather than like a repeated card template.
